Creamy Mushroom Noodles

A college student altered recipe of Gordon Ramsay's Mushroom, Leek and Tarragon Pasta. This recipe assumes the chef to be broke and on a budget. Feel free to replace certain aspects with other alternatives. Different protein like day old chicken wings or uneaten hamburger patties can be substituted. Free vegetables from college food programs and other "sources" can be used instead of leeks. Other unused pastas and soups can be used instead of the instant ramen. Any spices from the communal kitchen is encouraged to be appropriated and used.

  • Instant Ramen (soup packet used in sauce)
  • Vegetable (leek is used here)
  • Mushroom (any Agaricus bisporus can be used)
  • Tofu or any other protein
  • Water (or soup/broth if instant ramen is unavaliable)
  • Cream
  1. Chop mushrooms and vegetables to thin slices. Tofu can be cubed to any given size. 1/2 inch is recommended.
  2. Cook mushrooms untill cooked. Any method and consistency can be used however this method is recommended.
  3. Add in vegetables when water from mushrooms begins to evaporate.
  4. Add in soup and begin to simmer it and reduce.
  5. Add in a bit of cream to given consistency.
  6. Add in noodles and let simmer for 2 minutes. Add salt and pepper to taste.
